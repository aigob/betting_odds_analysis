Introduction

This project aims to analyze betting odds data from various bookmakers alongside the outcomes of Premier League matches (Season 2023/2024). The objective is to determine which bookmaker consistently offers the best value odds for different match outcomes.

The dataset includes match data such as the teams playing, scores, and several odds from multiple bookmakers (e.g., Bet365, BetVictor). By performing data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and comparisons, we will identify trends in the odds and determine if some bookmakers offer better odds consistently across the matches.

The dataset used in this project was sourced from Football-data.co.uk (https://www.football-data.co.uk/englandm.php).

Ultimately, this analysis will provide insights into which bookmakers offer the highest potential returns for bettors based on historical data.

Data Inspection and Cleaning Summary:

  1. Initial Dataset Structure:

    • The dataset initially contained 380 rows and 106 columns.
    • The dataset included columns representing match data (teams, results, etc.) and a variety of odds from different bookmakers.

  2. Missing Data:

    • Several columns had missing values, and columns with more than 10% missing data were removed.
      • Removed columns include:
        • Columns with bookmaker odds like IWH, IWD, IWA, and IWCH.
        • Some columns like MaxCAHH and MaxCAHA had very few missing values, but were also dropped due to the threshold.
    • After this step, the remaining dataset had 100 columns with much less missing data.

  3. Post-Cleaning Data Types and Missing Values:

    • The remaining columns after cleaning had the following types of missing values:
      • BWH, BWD, BWA, P>2.5, and others were identified as float64 and still had small amounts of missing data.
    • These missing values were filled using the median of each respective column.

  4. Key Columns for Analysis:

    • After cleaning, the key columns for the analysis include:
      • HomeTeam, AwayTeam, FTHG (Full-Time Home Goals), FTAG (Full-Time Away Goals) for match outcomes.
      • Betting odds columns such as B365H, B365D, B365A from bookmaker Bet365, along with others that survived the cleaning process.
    • These columns will be the focus of the analysis to determine which bookmaker provides the best value odds.

Data Preparation and Outlier Detection

Step 1: Data Selection and Filtering

To focus the analysis on the most relevant markets, all columns except for odds related to Home, Away, Draw, and Over/Under 2.5 goals per game were removed. The following bookmakers were included in the analysis: B365, BW, PS, P, WH, and VC.

Markets Considered:

  • Home Win (H)
  • Away Win (A)
  • Draw (D)
  • Over 2.5 Goals (>2.5)
  • Under 2.5 Goals (<2.5)

These columns were extracted and stored in a new DataFrame (league_HAD_OU). After organizing the columns, new columns were added to identify the maximum (Max_X) and minimum (Min_X) odds for each outcome (e.g., Home, Away, Draw, Over/Under 2.5). Additionally, the corresponding bookmakers offering the max and min odds for each outcome were recorded.

Step 2: Visualization with Boxplots for Outlier Detection

Boxplots were generated to visualize the distribution of odds for Home, Away, Draw, and Over/Under 2.5 goals across multiple bookmakers. Each boxplot shows the range of values (excluding outliers) and helps detect potential anomalies.

Below is the plot with boxplots for the maximum and minimum odds across different markets:

Analysis of Boxplots:

The boxplots above represent the distribution of maximum and minimum odds for each market (Home, Away, Draw, Over/Under 2.5 goals). Several key observations can be made:

  1. Max_Home & Min_Home: The odds for home wins display a relatively concentrated distribution, especially for the minimum odds, with few extreme outliers. The maximum odds have a wider spread, indicating greater variation between bookmakers in the highest odds offered.

  2. Max_Away & Min_Away: Similar to home odds, away odds show a wider range in maximum values, with several outliers extending above 15. These high odds likely correspond to weaker teams playing against stronger opponents, a common occurrence in football betting.

  3. Max_Draw & Min_Draw: Draw odds tend to be more stable, but outliers exist in both maximum and minimum odds. High draw odds, particularly in the Max_Draw column, could indicate matches where a draw is perceived as very unlikely by most bookmakers.

  4. Over/Under 2.5 Goals: The distribution of Over/Under odds shows fewer extreme outliers. This market is generally more stable, with odds clustering within expected ranges. However, some matches present odds over 4.0, which may indicate unique situations or perceived low probability of certain outcomes.

Discussion of Outliers:

While outliers are present in the boxplots, particularly in the Away and Draw markets, it is important to note that these outliers may not necessarily indicate data errors. In football betting, it is common to see high odds for underdogs, especially when weaker teams face much stronger ones. Therefore, these outliers can be seen as expected behavior rather than anomalies.

For example, when a weak team plays a strong team, bookmakers often offer high odds for the underdog (e.g., Max_Away or Max_Draw). These odds reflect the low probability of such outcomes, and thus, high odds do not always signify errors in the data but are instead a normal part of football odds generation.

In conclusion, while boxplots are helpful for detecting outliers, in the context of football odds, these outliers represent normal market behavior rather than data anomalies.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Step 1: Calculating Average Odds per Bookmaker

The first step in exploratory data analysis involved calculating the average odds offered by each bookmaker for various markets. This calculation excluded the maximum (Max_) and minimum (Min_) odds, as the focus was on identifying general trends in the average odds offered by each bookmaker. For each bookmaker and market (Home, Away, Draw, Over 2.5, and Under 2.5), the mean odds were computed and stored in a dictionary (averages).

Step 2: Grouping Average Odds by Market

After calculating the average odds, the next step involved grouping these averages by market. Initially, there was an issue where the market label 'H' (Home) was being incorrectly grouped with 'WHA' (Away) and 'WHD' (Draw) due to partial string matching. This caused odds for WHA and WHD to be grouped incorrectly under the Home market.

To fix this, the logic was updated to ensure that only the exact last character of the market label was matched for single-character markets like H, A, and D. This prevented incorrect grouping by ensuring that markets were only grouped if they exactly matched their intended label.

Step 3: Visualizing the Average Odds by Market and Bookmaker

With the average odds grouped by market, line plots were generated to visualize how different bookmakers compared across various markets. Each plot shows the bookmakers along the x-axis and their corresponding average odds on the y-axis. Separate line plots were created for the following markets:

  • Home (H)
  • Away (A)
  • Draw (D)
  • Over 2.5 Goals (>2.5)
  • Under 2.5 Goals (<2.5)

These visualizations provided an intuitive way to see which bookmaker offered the best odds for each market, allowing for easy comparison across bookmakers.

Key Findings from the Visualizations

  1. PS Offers the Highest Odds:

    • Across multiple markets (Home, Away, and Draw), PS consistently provided the highest average odds. However, the differences between PS and other bookmakers were relatively small.

  2. Close Competition in Over/Under Markets:

    • In the Over/Under 2.5 markets, the differences between bookmakers such as B365 and P2.5 were minimal. This suggests that these markets are highly competitive, with bookmakers offering very similar odds.

  3. Variability in the Away Market:

    • In the Away market, odds offered by bookmakers such as VCA and WHA showed more variability compared to other markets, suggesting that the Away market may present more opportunities for bettors looking for value.

This section of the EDA highlighted important insights from the data, including which bookmakers consistently offered the highest odds and how different markets compared in terms of variability and competitiveness.

def group_by_market(dict):
    grouped_dict = {}
    for key, value in dict.items():
        for item in required_markets:
            # Check the length of the market and match the last part of the string exactly
            if len(item) == 1:  # For 'H', 'A', 'D'
                if key[-1] == item:  # Match only the last character of the key
                    if item not in grouped_dict:
                        grouped_dict[item] = []  # Initialize an empty list if not present
                    grouped_dict[item].append((key, value))  # Append the bookmaker and odds as a tuple
            else:  # For '>2.5', '<2.5'
                if key[-len(item):] == item:  # Match exactly the last part of the string
                    if item not in grouped_dict:
                        grouped_dict[item] = []  # Initialize an empty list if not present
                    grouped_dict[item].append((key, value))  # Append the bookmaker and odds as a tuple
    return grouped_dict

Research Project Summary

This project aimed to analyze and compare betting odds data from various bookmakers for different Premier League football markets, including Home Win, Away Win, Draw, Over 2.5 goals, and Under 2.5 goals. The objective was to identify which bookmakers consistently offer the best value odds across different match outcomes. The following steps were performed in the analysis:

1. Data Inspection and Cleaning:

  • The dataset initially contained 380 rows and 106 columns, with a variety of columns related to match data and odds from different bookmakers.
  • Several columns had missing values, and those with more than 10% missing data were removed. This included columns like IWH, IWD, IWA, and others. A few columns such as MaxCAHH and MaxCAHA with sparse missing values were also dropped to maintain data quality.
  • After removing these columns, the dataset was reduced to 100 columns, significantly improving its usability.
  • For the remaining columns with small amounts of missing data, missing values were filled using the median of each column. This step ensured no missing data would interfere with the analysis.
  • After the cleaning process, the dataset was ready for analysis, containing relevant match outcomes, bookmaker odds, and the filled missing data.

2. Data Preparation and Filtering:

  • The cleaned dataset was filtered to include only the relevant columns related to the Home, Away, Draw, and Over/Under 2.5 goals markets.
  • New columns were added to identify the Max and Min odds for each market and the bookmakers offering those odds.
  • Outlier detection was performed using boxplots to visualize and discuss the distribution of odds, especially focusing on extreme values that could distort the analysis. Some outliers were considered normal due to the nature of football betting odds, where bookmakers often offer high odds for underdogs.

3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):

  • Average odds were calculated for each bookmaker in different markets to facilitate comparison.
  • The analysis also included grouping the odds by market and generating line charts to compare the odds provided by different bookmakers.
  • An issue related to misgrouping of market names (e.g., H being confused with WHA and WHD) was identified and fixed by adjusting the string-matching logic to ensure proper grouping.
  • Visualizations (line charts), were generated to explore trends in the odds and identify which bookmaker consistently offers better odds across various markets.

4. Final Findings:

  • After analyzing the data, it was found that PS offers the highest value odds in most markets. However, the margin of difference between PS and other bookmakers is relatively small.
  • The analysis concludes that, while PS might be the best option for maximizing returns in some cases, bettors should also consider other bookmakers, as differences in odds are not always substantial.
